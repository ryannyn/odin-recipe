Ingredients

  • 12 ounces (340g) penne pasta, or your favorite pasta shape
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup vodka
  • 1 can (28 ounces/800g) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for a bit of heat)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Grated Parmesan cheese, for serving

Instructions

  1. Cook the penne pasta according to the package instructions in a large pot of salted boiling water until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Pour in the vodka and let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the alcohol to cook off and the sauce to reduce slightly. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Add the heavy cream and red pepper flakes (if using). Simmer gently over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens, about 10-15 minutes. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  3. Add the cooked and drained penne pasta to the vodka sauce. Toss gently until the pasta is well coated with the sauce.
  4. Divide the pasta among serving plates or bowls. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese. Serve hot and enjoy your homemade vodka sauce pasta with a side of garlic bread or a simple green salad!
